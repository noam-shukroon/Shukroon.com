Half-Life

Half-Life (stylized as HλLF-LIFE) is a science fiction first-person shooter video game developed by Valve,
released in 1998 by Sierra Studios for Microsoft Windows. It was Valve's debut product and the first in the Half-Life series.
Players assume the role of Dr. Gordon Freeman,
who must fight his way out of a secret research facility after a teleportation experiment goes disastrously wrong,
fighting enemies and solving puzzles.

Developer: Valve.
Publishers: Sierra Studios + Valve Corporation (digital).
Writer: Marc Laidlaw.
Composer: Kelly Bailey, Ben Houge (PS2 main menu music)
Engine: GoldSrc.
Platforms: Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 2, OS X, Linux.
Release date: November 19, 1998.
Genre: First-person shooter.
Modes: Single-player, multiplayer.
